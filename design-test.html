Ground IX · Defender of the Bond Capitulation

The Defender did not perform the office
that Canon 1432 constitutes.

The Sentence at §31 records that the Defender of the Bond "cannot raise any arguments against the nullity of this marriage." FACT

Can. 1432 binds the Defender "by office to present and expound all that can reasonably be argued against the nullity." Dignitas Connubii Art. 56 §5 permits remittal to the justice of the court only when nothing can "reasonably be proposed or argued." ARGUMENT

A capitulation in a case where the record yielded the §1 and §2 admissions, the engagement photographs, the family-integration photographs, the Valerie voice-call archive, the November 2023 affectionate-call cluster, the 6/7 January 2024 outing six days before separation, and the 350 KWD post-separation support — is not a tenable judgement that "nothing reasonable" could be advanced for the bond. Moral certainty · Can. 1608 §1

An admission and a conclusion cannot stand together without a bridge.

The First Instance Sentence opens with two judicial admissions — that the nuptials "went off well" (§1) and that "after the marriage they appeared to be happy" (§2) — and closes (§30) with a finding that, at the moment described, both parties lacked the canonical capacity to assume the essential obligations of marriage.

Pius XII's allocution of 1 October 1942, ratified by Pope St John Paul II at the Rota in 1980, requires moral certainty that, "on the positive side, excludes well-founded or reasonable doubt." The Sentence does not bridge §1 and §2 to §30 by argument or expert testimony; it does not even acknowledge them in the §30 conclusion.

An internal contradiction of this gravity, unaddressed, falls short of the Can. 1608 §1 bar. The favour of the law (Can. 1060) requires that, in this doubt, the validity of the marriage of 28 August 2021 be upheld.
