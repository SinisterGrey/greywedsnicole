07 · Appeal

The Verso card

Each appeal ground is a card with two faces. Front is the legal argument. Click VERSO — the card flips like a postcard to reveal supporting evidence. Replaces the awkward desktop dual-panel and collapses cleanly on mobile.

Ground IV · Post-Factum Reasoning

The Sentence reasons backward
from post-marital fracture.

The First Instance Tribunal's finding under Can. 1095 §3 requires antecedent grave incapacity at the moment of consent. FACT

The Sentence itself admits the wedding "went off well." JUDICIAL ADMISSION This admission cannot coexist with a finding of radical antecedent incapacity. ARGUMENT

Pope Benedict XVI taught that the validity of the bond "does not depend on the subsequent conduct of the couple during their married life" (Rota, 29 January 2009). The reasoning is structurally inverted. ARGUMENT

Six days before the 13 January 2024 separation, the parties spent the day together. NEW EVIDENCE This conduct cannot be reconciled with permanent antecedent incapacity at consent two and a half years prior. INFERENCE
